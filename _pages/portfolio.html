Wei Xiangqing

I am currently a research assistant at Chiba University, Japan. My research focuses on AI accelerator architecture, reinforcement-learning-driven scheduling, and hardware-software co-design for neural network accelerators.

My current research interests include reinforcement learning based scheduling for NPU architectures, tile-level execution optimization for CNN accelerators, sparse neural network acceleration, and FPGA-based hardware validation. I am particularly interested in building intelligent scheduling frameworks that can optimize latency, energy efficiency, PE utilization, and memory access behavior for deep neural network workloads.

Research Experience

My recent work focuses on constructing a reinforcement-learning-driven scheduling framework for CNN accelerators. The framework models neural network workloads as tile-level directed acyclic graphs and uses reinforcement learning to optimize tile dispatch, PE assignment, memory reuse, and execution latency.

I have also worked on RTL-level modeling and simulation of NVDLA-style compute modules, including MAC arrays, PE utilization monitoring, tile-level execution traces, and reward-driven scheduling feedback for accelerator optimization.
